Philosophical Quotes



  1. The unexamined life is not worth living– Socrates



  2. The limits of my language mean the limit of my world.



  3. Entities should not be multiplied unnecessarily – William of Ockham



  4. The life of man (in a state of nature) is solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short – Thomas Hobbes



  5. I think therefore I am ( Cogito, ergo sum ) – René Descartes



  6. If you would be a real seeker after truth, it is necessary that at least once in your life you doubt, as far as possible, all things – René Descartes



  7. Happiness lies in virtuous activity, and perfect happiness lies in the best activity, which is contemplative – Aristotle



  8. I can control my passions and emotions if I can understand their nature – Spinoza



  9. Philosophers have hitherto only interpreted the world in various ways; the point, however, is to change it – Karl Marx



  10. It is wrong always, everywhere and for everyone, to believe anything upon insufficient evidence – W. K. Clifford



  11. Virtue is nothing else than right reason – Seneca the Younger



  12. Freedom is secured not by the fulfilling of one's desires, but by the removal of desire – Epictetus



  13. In everything, there is a share of everything – Anaxagoras



  14. A little philosophy inclineth man's mind to atheism; but depth in philosophy bringeth men’s minds about to religion – Sir Francis Bacon



  15. The brave man is he who overcomes not only his enemies but his pleasures – Democritus



  16. Good and evil, reward and punishment, are the only motives to a rational creature – John Locke



  17. To do as one would be done by, and to love one's neighbor as oneself, constitute the ideal perfection of utilitarian morality – John Stuart Mill



  18. Everything that exists is born for no reason, carries on living through weakness, and dies by accident – Jean-Paul Sartre



  19. Man is the measure of all things – Protagoras



  20. Man will never be free until the last king is strangled with the entrails of the last priest
