        • Ques. 1 In which year Nationalism was developed in France? What changes happened in France because of nationalism?
        Ans. Nationalism was developed in France in the year of 1789.

        => There were several changes because of development of Nationalism in France. They were as follows:

        1. Due to revolution there were constitutional and political changes.
        2. There was transfer of power from monarchy to a body of French citizens.
        3. The density of France was shaped by the French people.

        Ques.2 What were the measures taken by French Revolutionaries to create a sense of collective identity amongst French people?
        Ans.
        1. The idea of 'la patrie' (Fatherland) and 'le citoyen' (citizen) emphasized the notion of a united community enjoying equal rights within the constitution.
        2. In place former Royal Standard Flag, Tricolor Flag was chosen as the National Flag of France.
        3. The Estate Generals were replaced by the National Assembly.
        4. New hymns composed, oaths taken, martyrs commemorated.
        5. A centralised administrative system was introduced.
        6. A standard uniform system of weights and measures was introduced in France.
        7. Regional Dialects were discouraged and France was made the common language.

        Ques.3 Define Absolutist.
        Ans. The form of government in which all the powers are concentrated in the hands of a single person which is known as monarch and is who exercises control over the nation.

        Ques.4 Define Utopia.
        Ans. Utopia is the state of society being so ideal that is unlikely to actually exist.

        Ques.5 What do you know about Napoleon? Describe the civil code of Napoleon.

        => Napoleon Civil Code of Conduct also known as Napoleonic Code was introduced in France in the year 1804. Its objectives were:

        1. Napoleon introduced equality before the law.
        2. He abolished all the privileges based on birth.
        3. Right to Property was recognized.

        3. Right to property was recognized.
        4. Freed peasants from serfdom and manorial dues.
